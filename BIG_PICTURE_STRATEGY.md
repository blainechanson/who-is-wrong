# Big Picture Strategy

## Ultimate Goal

The larger goal is to build a series of websites and small apps that can grow meaningful audiences over time because they are unique, useful, entertaining, or otherwise value-adding.

The long-term aim is for these projects to become a portfolio of online assets that produce continual passive income as the years pass.

Who Is Wrong is the first project in that larger series.

## Origin Of The Idea

The strategy was inspired by watching examples of niche websites and online assets that build traffic, usefulness, and revenue over time. One source of inspiration is the Niche Pursuits YouTube channel:

https://www.youtube.com/@niche-pursuits

The early brainstorming around this goal led to the Who Is Wrong concept: a simple, memorable, shareable site with a clear entertainment hook and potential to attract organic search traffic, repeat use, social sharing, and sponsorship or ad revenue.

## Role Of Who Is Wrong

Who Is Wrong is intended to be the first proof-of-concept in the wider portfolio.

Its job is to test whether a small, focused web app can:

- Offer a distinctive user experience.
- Be simple enough to build and maintain.
- Attract traffic through SEO and sharing.
- Create repeatable engagement.
- Support monetization through sponsorships, ads, affiliates, or other lightweight revenue channels.
- Teach lessons that can be reused for future sites and apps.

## Portfolio Principles

Future projects should aim to share these traits:

- Clear concept that can be explained in one sentence.
- Unique angle or memorable hook.
- Real value, entertainment, utility, or novelty for users.
- Search-friendly pages and topics.
- Low operational complexity.
- Potential for compounding traffic over time.
- Monetization paths that do not ruin the user experience.
- Reusable technical and marketing patterns.

## What Success Looks Like

A successful project in this portfolio should become an asset that keeps working after the initial build.

Success may include:

- Growing organic search traffic.
- People sharing the site naturally.
- Repeat visitors or returning use cases.
- Email list, brand, or audience growth where appropriate.
- Sponsorship, advertising, affiliate, product, or subscription revenue.
- Clear learnings that make the next project faster and better.

## Important Mindset

Who Is Wrong should be treated as both a real product and a learning vehicle.

The goal is not only to make this one site work. The goal is to discover repeatable patterns for building small, valuable online properties that can compound into a durable passive-income portfolio over time.
