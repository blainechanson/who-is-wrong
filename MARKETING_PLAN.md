# Who Is Wrong Marketing Plan

## Strategic Goal

Make Who Is Wrong grow quietly through the product itself.

The aim is not to build a public personal brand, collect phone numbers, collect email addresses, or become active on social media. The aim is to build a website that is useful, funny, searchable, and shareable enough that it can self-attract traffic after a small, careful introduction to the internet.

## Core Marketing Idea

The website should be the marketing object.

Who Is Wrong should grow through four quiet engines:

1. Search traffic from people already looking for ways to settle arguments, judge petty disputes, or find funny AI tools.
2. Shareable certificates that quietly carry the site name and domain.
3. Useful category pages that act like mini tools, not just SEO articles.
4. One-time launch seeding in a few relevant places, then letting the product carry itself.

## Positioning

One-sentence positioning:

> A funny AI judge for harmless arguments.

The site should be described as:

- a funny AI judge
- a mock-serious courtroom for petty disputes
- an unofficial verdict generator
- a playful way to settle harmless arguments

The site should not be described as:

- mediation
- arbitration
- legal judgement
- counselling
- advice
- truth-finding

## Growth Loop

The main growth loop should be:

1. Someone has a harmless argument.
2. They use Who Is Wrong.
3. They receive a funny official decree.
4. They share the certificate or screenshot.
5. Someone else sees `whoiswrong.ai`.
6. That person tries their own case.

This means the certificate is not just an output. It is the viral unit.

## Certificate Requirements

Every certificate should:

- be readable on phones
- look good in screenshots and shares
- include `Who Is Wrong`
- include `whoiswrong.ai`
- include an entertainment-only disclaimer
- avoid visible UI controls inside the generated image
- work for short, medium, and long verdicts without awkward footer spacing

The certificate should make people curious enough to ask, "Where did you make that?"

## Pages To Strengthen Before Launch

The current category pages should become useful mini tools:

- Relationship Debates
- Family Arguments
- Workplace Nonsense
- Friend Group Drama
- Household Disputes
- Everyday Arguments
- Settle An Argument

Each page should have:

- a clear funny promise
- examples people immediately understand
- case generator buttons
- a direct path back to the judge
- useful copy for search engines without feeling bloated

## Search Strategy

Target searches where people already have the problem or curiosity:

- settle an argument online
- who is wrong in this argument
- funny argument judge
- AI judge for fun
- petty dispute resolver
- relationship argument judge
- family argument generator
- workplace argument generator
- friend group drama judge
- roommate or household dispute judge

Search pages should be useful first. Avoid thin pages made only to rank.

## Future Example Verdict Pages

Add fictional, non-user-submitted example verdict pages.

Examples:

- The Pizza Topping Trial
- The Group Chat Reply Case
- The Hoodie Ownership Hearing
- The Dishwasher Loading Dispute
- The Family Leftovers Case
- The Meeting That Could Have Been A Message

These pages can show what the product does, attract search traffic, and give visitors a fast way to try a similar case.

Important: do not publish real user cases.

## Low-Key Launch Plan

### Phase 1: Technical Launch

- Submit sitemap in Google Search Console.
- Submit sitemap in Bing Webmaster Tools.
- Confirm all important pages are indexable.
- Confirm page titles and meta descriptions are unique.
- Confirm mobile experience is strong.
- Confirm OG/social preview image looks good.
- Confirm certificate sharing works on Android, iPhone, and desktop.

### Phase 2: Careful Seeding

Do not mass post. Do not spam. Do not pretend to be a community member just to drop links.

Possible places for one careful introduction:

- Product Hunt
- small AI tool directories
- fun website directories
- indie maker directories
- relevant Reddit communities, only where it genuinely fits
- small newsletters or roundups about useful/funny web tools

The launch message should be honest and simple:

> I made a tiny AI judge for harmless arguments. It turns petty disputes into funny mock courtroom verdicts.

### Phase 3: Passive Growth

After launch, focus on:

- improving pages that get impressions
- adding useful category pages
- improving certificate share quality
- watching which use cases naturally attract traffic
- building only the pages that match real demand

## Tiny Paid Test

Only after the site feels polished, test a small paid budget.

Suggested test:

> $50-$100 test on Reddit ads targeting relationship humor, family humor, and workplace humor.

Other small tests:

- $50 exact-match search ad test for "settle an argument online"
- $50 sponsorship or placement in a small "cool websites" newsletter
- $50 test in an AI tools directory if the audience fits

Judge paid tests by actual site use, not just clicks.

## Monetization Strategy

The site should prioritize growth and trust before aggressive monetization. The product works best when it feels funny, fast, and shareable, so monetization should not interrupt the core judge flow or make the site feel spammy.

### Recommended Order

1. Launch and grow usage first.
2. Use sponsor slots as the first monetization path.
3. Add tasteful advertising later only if traffic is large enough to justify it.
4. Consider affiliate links only where they are genuinely relevant and harmless.

### Sponsorships

Sponsorships should be the preferred first revenue model because they can feel more premium and brand-aligned than random display ads.

Possible sponsor categories:

- party games
- card games
- novelty gifts
- funny apps or tools
- food delivery, snacks, or casual entertainment brands
- harmless dating, relationship, or social-humor brands
- AI tools that fit the playful audience

Avoid sponsors that make the site feel serious, unsafe, or off-brand:

- law firms or legal advice services
- therapy, counselling, or mediation services
- gambling
- adult content
- political campaigns or political advocacy
- debt, finance, or high-stakes advice products

Possible sponsor language:

> Court Sponsor: [Brand Name]

Sponsor placements should stay outside the generated certificate image. The certificate should remain a clean viral asset for the site itself.

### Advertising

Display advertising can be a later backfill revenue source if traffic becomes meaningful, but it should be added carefully.

Better places for ads:

- lower content sections
- category pages
- FAQ or educational content areas
- bottom banner areas

Avoid ads:

- inside the certificate
- between form fields
- beside or inside the verdict
- anywhere that damages screenshots, sharing, or the feeling of the joke

### Long-Term Revenue Mix

The ideal future model is:

- primary revenue: direct sponsors
- secondary revenue: tasteful display ads on content and category pages
- optional revenue: relevant affiliate links

The guiding rule is that monetization should support the site's growth loop, not weaken it. The certificate, form, and verdict experience should remain clean, fast, and easy to share.

## What Not To Do

Avoid:

- collecting emails just because marketing advice says to
- collecting phone numbers
- building a public personal brand around the site
- mass posting links on Reddit
- creating fake hype
- publishing real user cases
- making hundreds of thin AI-generated SEO pages
- adding intrusive ads too early
- making the site feel serious, legal, or therapeutic

## Best Next Work

Before public launch, complete a full pre-launch audit:

1. Search-readiness audit.
2. Share/certificate viral-loop audit.
3. Category-page improvement list.
4. Launch checklist.
5. First 20 SEO/page ideas ranked by likely value.

The goal is not to do everything. The goal is to choose the small set of changes most likely to increase organic discovery and sharing.
